# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Noivas & Cia is used primarily by store attendants and administrators during daily commercial operations for clothing and accessory rentals. Attendants work at the counter and need fast, low-friction screens for customers, rentals, pickups, returns, receivables, payments, and availability checks. Administrators manage inventory, company settings, users, permissions, reports, and controlled maintenance. Public visitors use the website only as a secondary entry point to understand the company and access signup or login.

## Product Purpose

The product replaces a legacy Windows 98 desktop system with a centralized Django web application that runs in a browser. It exists to keep rental operations reliable, reduce data-loss risk, shorten service time, and make customer, inventory, contract, movement, billing, and reporting workflows consistent across the store. Success means staff can complete operational tasks quickly, with clear state, few clicks, strong data integrity, and no dependence on outdated hardware or operating systems.

## Brand Personality

Reliable, efficient, professional. The interface should feel calm, direct, and work-focused: polished enough for a bridal and events business, but never decorative at the expense of speed or clarity. The voice should be concrete, concise, and in Brazilian Portuguese for all user-facing text.

## Anti-references

Avoid playful or marketing-first visuals, generic SaaS or AI-looking UI, excessive gradients, glassmorphism, decorative animations, low contrast, and anything that makes operational tasks slower or less clear. Avoid visual treatments that compete with forms, tables, status, totals, due dates, permissions, or the primary action on each screen.

## Design Principles

1. Operational clarity first: each screen should make the current task, current state, and next action obvious.
2. Fast counter workflows: reduce unnecessary choices, repeated typing, and visual friction in rentals, returns, payments, and availability checks.
3. Professional restraint: use the bridal/event identity as a controlled accent, not as decoration that weakens readability.
4. Consistent system behavior: forms, tables, alerts, navigation, permissions, and destructive actions should behave predictably across modules.
5. Data confidence: important business values such as customer identity, rental dates, item status, balances, overdue amounts, and permissions must be easy to scan and hard to misread.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA. Use strong contrast, keyboard-friendly interfaces, visible focus states, clear labels, reduced-motion support, and layouts that remain usable on desktop, tablet, and mobile. Do not rely on color alone for status or errors.
